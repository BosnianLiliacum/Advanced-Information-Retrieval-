  Author: Sevynz13
  Comment: I got it all back! Here's what I did:  
Put all drives back in their original drive bays.  
Booted up to the HP Smart Array Utility and created a new array exactly as the old array was.  
Rebooted and it booted up to VMWare again, but the main datastore was missing with all the VMs on it.  
I then SSH'd into VMware and ran some commands [following the directions from here](https://sahlitech.com/mounting-vmfs-datastore-esxi/) to remount the datastore.   
Then in VMware I went to Storage &gt; Devices &gt; Clicked "Rescan" then "Refresh" but it still didn't come up.  
Then I refreshed my browser and there was my datastore.  
I then booted up all the VMs so far with no problems.

In case that link ever dies here are the commands:  
esxcfg-volume -l     *Shows volume label*  
esxcfg-volume -M volumelabel      *Mounts specified volume*
